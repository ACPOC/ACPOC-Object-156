Overview

The Soviet Extinction Event of 1951 is the name given to the series of deaths caused in the U.S.S.R. by the Soviet Genocide Virus throughout 1951. It is commonly pointed to as the end of the Cold War between the United States and Soviet Union, due to the fact that it led to the complete destruction of the Soviet Union's population. What is particularly unique about this event is that it was caused by the first, and only as of June 2024, virus in the world that is able to specifically target a certain ethnicity. Due to this, many theorize to this day that the Soviet Genocide Virus was created in an American funded lab and used as weapon to specifically engineer the destruction of the Soviet Union.

Aftermath

Many historians deem the Soviet Exctinction Event of 1951, and the end of the Cold War, as the starting point of the American Utopian Revolution. Prior to the event, billions of tax-payer dollars were being invested into the development of war technology, most notably the development of atomic and hydrogen bombs and their delivery mechanisms.
